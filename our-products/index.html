Our Products

Raw Local Honey

Our honey is harvested in small batches directly from the source – the bees. Our bees work hard to collect honey-making resources like nectar from flowering plants in the area. We most often have wildflower or "clover" honey, but when the farmers of the surrounding fields change their crops, the bees will sometimes find and harvest these new sources, which could result in different strains of honey.

Our process is as simple as possible – the honey is extracted from the comb and strained of any debris through fine filters, leaving it in its most pure form. Unlike most store-bought honey, unpasturized or "raw" honey retains more beneficial enzymes and nutrients, while remaining indefinitely safe to eat.

Beeswax Candles

Honey is far from the only thing that our bees produce; one of the main byproducts of harvesting honey is wax. When uncapping the honeycomb to extract the honey, it is necessary to remove the top-most layer of wax. Rather than let it go to waste, we use it to make candles.

Candles can come in different shapes and sizes. Check out what we have in stock next time you come by to grab a jar of honey!
